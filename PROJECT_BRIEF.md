# Project Brief: Lilya's by Cybel (eatlilyas.com)

A comprehensive overview of the client, vision, and technical requirements for the Lilya's by Cybel website project.

---

## 1. High-Level Summary

- **Client:** Lilya's by Cybel (a consumer-facing brand under the parent company, Cybel Fusion).
- **Project:** Build their first-ever website for the domain eatlilyas.com.
- **Core Goal:** Establish a polished online presence for the Lilya's brand in Florida. The key challenge is to create a dual-purpose site that serves two distinct audiences: wholesale B2B clients and direct-to-consumer (D2C) retail customers.

---

## 2. Business Model & Website Functionality

The website must be designed to support two separate business operations:

### Wholesale Portal (B2B)

- **Product Focus:** Primarily for salads and other pre-packaged foods.
- **Audience:** Businesses across Florida (cafes, markets, corporate offices) looking to stock Lilya's products.
- **Required Functionality:** This will likely need a password-protected area or a specific application/onboarding process. Features could include bulk ordering, wholesale-specific pricing, and delivery information.

### Retail E-commerce (D2C)

- **Product Focus:** Primarily for their line of teas, with a special emphasis on the "Sip & Go" products.
- **Audience:** Health-conscious individual consumers in Florida.
- **Required Functionality:** A standard, user-friendly e-commerce experience including product pages, a shopping cart, and online checkout for shipping within Florida.

---

## 3. Brand Identity & Voice

This is a core pillar of the project. The client was very clear on the brand's perception.

- **Primary Directive:** The brand should represent "a healthy lifestyle without mentioning the word 'healthy'." The focus should be on flavor, freshness, and quality.

### Brand Voice Keywords

- Fresh
- Elevated
- Approachable
- Vibrant
- Clean
- Flavorful

> The overall feeling should be premium but not unapproachable. It's about conscious living and enjoying high-quality, delicious food and drinks.

---

## 4. Target Audience

### Retail Consumers

- **Who:** Health-conscious individuals and families across Florida.
- **Challenge they face:** A "lack of good pre-packaged food" that is both convenient and flavorful.
- **What they want:** Easy access to delicious, fresh-tasting teas and foods that fit their "conscious" lifestyle.

### Wholesale Buyers

- **Who:** Business owners and managers of cafes, health food stores, gyms, and corporate offices.
- **What they want:** A reliable supplier of high-quality, pre-packaged food (like salads) that their customers will love. They need an easy ordering process and consistent quality.

---

## 5. Design & Visual Direction

### Color Palette

The client specified a clear direction: **GREEN**, **WHITE**, and **BLUE** with an "earthy, healthy" feel.

**Suggested Interpretation:**

- **Greens:** Earthy, natural tones (sage, olive) to represent fresh ingredients.
- **Whites:** Crisp, clean off-whites to create a feeling of spaciousness and purity.
- **Blues:** Cool, calming blues that can evoke water, freshness, and the Florida coast.

### Mood & Vibe

Clean, modern, vibrant, and natural. The design should feel airy and light. High-quality photography of the ingredients and final products will be essential.

### Primary Design Inspiration: DrinkKoia.com

**Why it works:** Koia's site is a fantastic model. It features a clean layout, vibrant product photography that pops against a simple background, and clear messaging that focuses on ingredients and benefits without being overly clinical. We should aim for this level of polish.

### Competitor to Reference: Lemonicious

This competitor should be reviewed to understand the local market and identify opportunities for Lilya's to differentiate itself.

---

## 6. Key Content & Messaging Topics

The website copy and content should revolve around these themes:

- Flavorful teas and fresh ingredients
- Convenient, pre-packaged foods
- Wholesale food service opportunities
- Retail beverages (Sip & Go Teas)
- Distribution throughout Florida
- Conscious living and a better lifestyle

---

## 7. Suggestions & Next Steps

Given the prebuild_planning status, here are some actionable next steps:

1. **Develop a Sitemap:** Map out the site structure. How will users navigate between the retail and wholesale sections? Suggestion: a clear menu with "Shop Our Teas" (Retail) and "Wholesale Partners" (B2B).

2. **Define User Flows:** Create a flow for a retail customer (land on site → browse teas → add to cart → checkout) and a wholesale buyer (land on site → navigate to wholesale → fill out an application form → access portal).

3. **Create Forms:** A key element will be the wholesale application form. Fields needed: business name, contact info, business type, and estimated order volume.

4. **Set Up Endpoints:** Once the form is designed, route all wholesale inquiries to the client's preferred email address.
